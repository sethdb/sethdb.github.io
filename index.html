Pokemon: Rupture


Pokemon: Rupture, is a fanmade ROM hack created using the Pokemon Essentials Toolkit alongside RPG Maker XP.
The story follows you, the player, along your journey as you take on rivals, earn gym badges, and battle with wise sages. With the help of a mysterious Pokemon, this open world adventure gives you the choice to progress as you please, thanks to a level-scaling system that applies to all game NPCs. Explore the vast mountains, relax in the massive Soul City, or take on the head of the most powerful corporation in the land to stop greed and pollution. The choice is up to you.
Game currently in early stages of development. Attached art and gameplay are subject to change. Keep an eye out for future updates.

Pokemon, but Java


Pokemon, but Java, is a personal attempt at recreating the iconic and complex combat system of the Pokemon video game series. With hundreds of invidual Pokemon out there, each using a specific set of 6 different stat categories, this project lends itself well to Object Oriented Languages such as Java. Using it as a teaching tool, this project must find a way to include numerous variables for each individual pokemon, some entirely non- quantifiable. Thanks to the flexibility of OOL's, this unique combat system can be reverse engineered and ported to a different language


Stay tuned for a dedicated website with links to documentation, downloads, and source code.

Keysight Technologies Competitive Analysis


During my internship with Keysight Technologies' Digital Demand Generation team in 2018, I gathered data on the social media pages of Keysight and its top five competitors in the industry. Using social listening tools such as Synthesio, and my own intution, I analyzed where Keysight fell in the totem pole of social media, judging them on a variety of factors such as: follower count, post quantity, post consistency, follower interaction, and growth over time, among others.

After 2 months of collecting this data, and formulating my recommendations on areas for improvement and strength, I presented my findings to the entire global Digital Demand Generation team.

Brometheus Imaging


As a part of my business management capstone class for my undergraduate degree, the class was split up into 12 teams of 4-5 students, who competed against each other in a 15-week business simulation. Each team was entered into the same industry of drones and high end cameras, where we competed to achieve the highest number of points in the simulation. Given full control to the inner workings of a company - finance, marketing, manufacturing, supply chain, etc. - we were judged on how much we improved from week to week (each week being 1 year in the simulation).

As co-leader of team Brometheus Imaging, I was in charge of all marketing operations. Using the data given in the simulation, it was my job to decide where we would decide our marketing budget: which regions and demographics to focus on, using or not using product promotions, the marketing channels that would best reach our top consumers, product pricing and manufacturing quality. By the end of the 15-week competition, Brometheus Imaging came out on top, placing first out of the 12 teams in the class.

Additionally, thanks to our high score, we were invited to compete in the 2019 GLO-BUS Best-Strategy Invitational, where the top teams from 10+ universities across the world would continue to compete, extending the simulation, striving for ultimate victory.
